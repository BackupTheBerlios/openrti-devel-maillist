> On Fri, 15 Feb 2013 07:44:13 +0100
> Mathias Fröhlich <Mathias.Froehlich at gmx.net> wrote:
> Thanx Mathias for your help all works, but in my situation
> ./fddget -O fdd.xml -o ObjectClass0 -a Attribute0 protocol=rti
> address=localhost:1234 could you please explain how can I use
> rti://<host>/<path>/<model> argument? Thanx in advance

You can put that into the federation execution name.
For fddget this is the
-F rti://localhost//FederationName
argument.

The rationale behind is that rti13 does not have any way to give connect time 
arguments to the rti. Therefore I decided to allow federation execution names 
to be urls including this type of information.

The rti1516e implementation behaves different in this respect. This is since 
there is an explicit connect call.
